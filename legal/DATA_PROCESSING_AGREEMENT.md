## 1. Introduction and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This DPA governs the processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the WhatsApp SaaS Platform service ("Service").

### 1.2 Incorporation

This DPA is incorporated into and forms part of the Terms of Service between Controller and Processor.

### 1.3 Hierarchy

In case of conflict, the order of precedence is:
1. This DPA
2. Standard Contractual Clauses (if applicable)
3. Terms of Service
4. Privacy Policy

### 1.4 Definitions

**Personal Data**: Information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined in applicable Data Protection Laws.

**Data Protection Laws**: GDPR, CCPA, and all other applicable privacy and data protection laws and regulations.

**GDPR**: Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council.

**Processing**: Any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion.

**Data Subject**: The individual to whom Personal Data relates.

**Supervisory Authority**: An independent public authority established by an EU Member State.

**Sub-processor**: Any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data.

## 2. Processing of Personal Data

### 2.1 Roles

- **Controller** determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data
- **Processor** processes Personal Data on behalf of and according to Controller's instructions
- Processor acts as a Processor, not a Controller, of Customer Data

### 2.2 Controller's Instructions

Processor shall process Personal Data only:
- In accordance with Controller's documented instructions
- As necessary to provide the Service
- As required by applicable law

The Service functionality constitutes Controller's documented instructions.

### 2.3 Unauthorized Instructions

If Processor believes an instruction violates Data Protection Laws:
- Processor will immediately inform Controller
- Processor may suspend execution until legality is confirmed
- Controller indemnifies Processor for following its instructions

### 2.4 Processor's Personnel

Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data:
- Are subject to confidentiality obligations
- Receive appropriate training on data protection
- Process data only as instructed by Controller

## 3. Nature and Purpose of Processing

### 3.1 Subject Matter

Processing of Personal Data through the WhatsApp SaaS Platform messaging service.

### 3.2 Duration

Processing occurs for the duration of the Service agreement and 30 days thereafter for data retrieval, unless earlier deletion is requested.

### 3.3 Nature of Processing

- Collection
- Storage
- Organization
- Structuring
- Retrieval
- Use
- Transmission
- Disclosure
- Deletion

### 3.4 Purpose of Processing

- Providing the WhatsApp messaging service
- Managing customer conversations
- Storing conversation history
- Providing analytics and reporting
- Providing technical support

### 3.5 Categories of Data Subjects

- Controller's customers (End Users)
- Controller's employees and representatives
- Other individuals communicating through the Service

### 3.6 Types of Personal Data

- **Contact Information**: Phone numbers, names, email addresses
- **Communication Data**: Message content, timestamps, metadata

## 4. Controller's Obligations

### 4.1 Legal Basis

Controller represents and warrants that:
- It has a legal basis for processing Personal Data
- It has obtained necessary consents from Data Subjects
- Its instructions comply with Data Protection Laws
- It will provide Privacy Notices to Data Subjects

### 4.2 Data Subject Rights

Controller is responsible for:
- Responding to Data Subject requests (access, deletion, etc.)
- Providing required notices to Data Subjects
- Handling Data Subject complaints
- Maintaining records of consent and legal basis

### 4.3 Data Accuracy

Controller shall:
- Ensure Personal Data is accurate and up-to-date
- Provide mechanisms for Data Subjects to update their data
- Promptly correct or delete inaccurate data

## 5. Processor's Obligations

### 5.1 Confidentiality

Processor shall maintain confidentiality of all Personal Data and process it only as instructed by Controller.

### 5.2 Security Measures

Processor implements appropriate technical and organizational measures including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256-GCM)
- Access controls and authentication
- Firewall and intrusion detection
- Regular security testing and audits
- Automated security monitoring
- Incident response procedures

**Organizational Measures:**
- Security policies and procedures
- Employee training and awareness
- Background checks for sensitive roles
- Non-disclosure agreements
- Access reviews and audits
- Business continuity planning
- Disaster recovery procedures

See Annex 2 for detailed security measures.

### 5.3 Assistance with Data Subject Rights

Processor shall assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests:
- Access requests
- Rectification requests
- Erasure requests ("right to be forgotten")
- Data portability requests
- Restriction of processing requests
- Objection requests

**Process:**
- Data Subject contacts Controller directly
- Controller submits request to Processor via support@example.com
- Processor responds within 10 business days
- Controller is responsible for final response to Data Subject

**Fees:**
- First 5 requests per year: No charge
- Additional requests: $100 per request (Professional), $0 (Enterprise)

### 5.4 Assistance with Security and Compliance

Processor shall assist Controller with:
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Consultations with Supervisory Authorities
- Security assessments and audits
- Compliance with Data Protection Laws

### 5.5 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a Personal Data breach:

**Notification:**
- Processor will notify Controller without undue delay
- Notification within 72 hours of discovery
- Via email to Controller's designated contact

**Information Provided:**
- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

**Cooperation:**
- Processor will cooperate with Controller's investigation
- Processor will provide reasonable assistance
- Controller is responsible for notifying Supervisory Authorities and Data Subjects as required by law

## 8. Data Retention and Deletion

### 8.1 Retention Period

Personal Data is retained:
- While Service agreement is active
- For 30 days after account closure (for recovery)
- As required by law
- As requested by Controller

### 8.2 Deletion

Upon Controller's request or termination of Service:
- Processor will delete or return all Personal Data
- Deletion completed within 30 days
- Certificate of deletion provided upon request

### 8.3 Legal Retention

Processor may retain Personal Data if required by law:
- Processor will inform Controller of retention requirement
- Data will be securely stored and isolated
- Data will be deleted when legal requirement ends

### 8.4 Backup Retention

Personal Data in backups is deleted:
- Within 90 days after deletion from production systems
- Backups are encrypted and access-restricted

## 9. Audit Rights

### 9.1 Audit Frequency

- Controller may audit Processor's compliance annually
- Additional audits if required by Data Protection Laws
- Additional audits following a data breach

### 9.2 Audit Scope

Audits may cover:
- Technical and organizational security measures
- Sub-processor management
- Data Subject request procedures
- Breach notification procedures
- Compliance with this DPA

### 9.3 Audit Process

**Standard Audit:**
- Processor provides SOC 2 Type II report (annual)
- Processor completes Controller's security questionnaire
- No charge for standard audit

**On-Site Audit:**
- 60 days' advance notice required
- Conducted during business hours
- Maximum 2 days duration
- Does not interfere with Processor's operations
- Costs borne by Controller

**Third-Party Audit:**
- Controller may engage third-party auditor
- Auditor must sign NDA
- Processor may refuse auditor with reasonable justification

### 9.4 Audit Reports

- Audit findings shared confidentially
- Processor will remediate issues within reasonable timeframe
- Critical issues remediated within 30 days

## 11. Liability and Indemnification

### 11.1 Liability Cap

Processor's total liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations set forth in the Terms of Service.

### 11.2 Data Protection Liability

Processor is liable for damages caused by:
- Processing in violation of Data Protection Laws
- Acting outside or contrary to Controller's instructions
- Failure to implement appropriate security measures

### 11.3 Joint Liability (GDPR)

Under GDPR Article 82:
- Controller and Processor may be jointly liable for damages
- Each party liable only for damages it caused
- Party that pays full compensation may recover from other party

### 11.4 Indemnification

**Processor Indemnifies Controller** for claims arising from:
- Processor's violation of Data Protection Laws
- Processor's breach of this DPA
- Sub-processor's violations (to extent Processor is responsible)

**Controller Indemnifies Processor** for claims arising from:
- Controller's instructions violating Data Protection Laws
- Controller's lack of legal basis for processing
- Controller's failure to obtain necessary consents

## 12. Term and Termination

### 12.1 Term

This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the Service agreement.

### 12.2 Termination

This DPA terminates:
- Upon termination of Service agreement
- 30 days after all Personal Data is deleted
- As otherwise agreed by parties

### 12.3 Survival

The following provisions survive termination:
- Confidentiality obligations
- Liability and indemnification
- Audit rights (for 12 months post-termination)
- Data deletion obligations
